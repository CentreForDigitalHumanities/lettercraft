Glossary

In studying narrative descriptions of epistolary communication, the Lettercraft team has identified dozens of Latin words that have something to do with letters and their use. One substantial segment of this vocabulary refer to letters themselves; other clusters refer to the people carrying them, and to accompanying gifts; the potentially largest and most fluid segment refers to the wider communicative process in which letters functioned, including terms associated with the acts of writing, dictating, signing, reading, carrying, speaking, gift-giving, negotiating and hosting.

One of the aims of the Lettercraft project has been to map out this terminology so as to gain a better understanding of its meaning and use. To this end, all entries in the database contain a ‘designator’ field that gives the relevant Latin epistolary terminology that appears in the passage concerned. These terms can be searched with the search function. A full terminology list, with frequencies, can be downloaded for individual texts and for the database at large.

To help navigate this part of the database, we have created a glossary of the most important terms one might encounter. Each entry gives the key meanings of the term from antiquity up to the Merovingian period and gives attestations in the Merovingian narrative corpus. For further context, entries might refer to a broad range of ancient and medieval sources as well as modern scholarship. The bibliography at the end offers a full list of relevant primary sources, dictionaries, and modern publications.
