Glossary

In studying narrative descriptions of epistolary communication, the Lettercraft team has identified hundreds of Latin words that have something to do with letters and their use. One substantial segment of this vocabulary refer to letters themselves; others clusters refers to the people carrying them, and to accompanying gifts; the largest and most fluid segment refers to the wider communicative process in which letters functioned, including terms associated with the acts of writing, dictating, signing, reading, carrying, speaking, gift-giving, negotiating and hosting.

One of the aims of the Lettercraft project has been to map out this terminology so as to gain a better understanding of its meaning and use. To this end, all entries in the database contain a ‘designator’ field that gives the relevant Latin epistolary terminology that appears in the passage concerned. These terms can be searched with the search function. A full terminology list, with frequencies, can be downloaded for individual texts and for the database at large.
